*By observing the past, we test the future. By knowing others, we know ourselves.*

    - **Falsifier:** If the hawkish-to-dovish word count ratio is >2:1 over the next 10 trading days, this dovish assumption may be premature.

2)  **Optimism quality:** You're likely betting that current optimism, especially in AI, is rooted in genuine productivity gains (like mid-2005) and can withstand macro pressure, rather than being a fragile bet on a Fed pivot that could evaporate (like late-2007).
    - **Test:** Track the performance of non-profitable tech (e.g., ARKK) versus high-quality, cash-flow-positive tech (e.g., MSFT, GOOGL) over the FP-R window.
    - **Falsifier:** If non-profitable tech outperforms high-quality tech by >5% during a period of rising yields, it suggests optimism is speculative, not fundamental.

3)  **Single decisive indicator:** Your focus on AI earnings and Fed-speak suggests you believe the inflation trajectory is the most decisive variable, as it dictates the Fed's ability to accommodate growth.
    - **Test:** Compare the market's reaction (30-min price change in SPY) to the next CPI release versus the next Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) release.
    - **Falsifier:** If the absolute market reaction to NFP is >1.5x the reaction to CPI, it implies the market is now more concerned with a growth slowdown (labor) than inflation.
