## 3.2 Multi-Scenario Projection (strictly historical-based, not a forecast)
- **Negative Scenario (Ref: 2018-09, 2007-07):**
*FP-L: Apparent stability at the peak masks macro fragility. → FP-R: A policy shock triggers a rapid de-risking.*
The mechanism here is that the market’s low-volatility complacency is shattered when a macro threat—either a hawkish Fed sticking to its guns or a geopolitical shock like tariffs—proves more impactful than the AI narrative. Capital flees from crowded growth leadership to safety, and the correction is amplified by the unwind of leveraged positions.
**Switch trigger:** if the 2-year Treasury yield rises >20 bps within any 5-day period during FP-R, downgrade path to a deeper correction.

- **Neutral Scenario (Ref: 2017-03):**
*FP-L: Cross-currents of tech optimism and policy noise. → FP-R: A low-volatility grind as neither bull nor bear case dominates.*
In this path, the powerful secular AI trend provides a durable floor for sentiment and valuations, preventing a sharp sell-off. Simultaneously, uncertainty around tariffs and Fed policy provides a ceiling, preventing a breakout. The market becomes trapped in a well-defined range, frustrating both bulls and bears as it awaits a decisive catalyst.
**Switch trigger:** if the S&P 500 fails to make a new high but holds above its 50-day moving average for the entirety of FP-R, the neutral path is confirmed; a break in either direction upgrades/downgrades the path.

- **Positive Scenario (Ref: 2019-06, 1995-10):**
*FP-L: A healthy pause before the next leg up. → FP-R: The dominant narrative overwhelms macro fears, forcing a breakout.*
The mechanism is a narrative feedback loop. Capital continues to pour into the perceived secular winners (AI), driving prices higher. This price action is then interpreted as confirmation of the theme's strength, attracting more capital. Any hint of a dovish policy shift or deferral of tariff threats acts as an accelerant, forcing sidelined cash into the market.
**Switch trigger:** if the Nasdaq 100 outperforms the S&P 500 by >3% during the first two weeks of FP-R, upgrade path to a potential melt-up.
